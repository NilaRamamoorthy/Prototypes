Your guide to opening a Korean Kitchen for delivery success

Korean culture has seen an explosive rise in global popularity, largely fueled by K-pop, K-dramas, and movies like Parasite, The United States, in particular, has embraced this cultural wave, leading to a surge demand for authentic Korean food.

Why start a korean kitchen for delivery?

  • Lower costs & higher profits- Without the need for a dine-in-space, expenses on rent, decor, and waitstafff are drastically reduced
  • Efficiency & convinience- A ghost kitchen model focuses solely on delivery,allowing streamlined operations and faster service.
  • Expanding market opportunity - Korean food is no longer niche;it's mainstrean.From college students craving korean fried chicken to health-concious consumers looking for kinchi-based probiotic meals, your target audience is vast.

Steps to start a Korean kitchen for delivery


Research and plan your business

  1. Conduct market research to identify your target audience and understand local demand for Korean cuisine
  2. Study competitors to identify gaps in the market and unique selling points
  3. Develop a solid business plan covering financial projections,sourcing authentic ingredienys,pricing strategies, anmd marketing efforts.


Choose the right kitchen model

  1. A ghost kitchen model is an excellent choice for delivery-only operations, reducing overhead costs and allowing for efficient service.
  2. Partnering with a ghost kitchen providr like CloudKitchens offers benefits such as pre-equipped kitchens, streamlined order management,and options to integrate delivery services.
